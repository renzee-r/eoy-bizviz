Actions: PRODUCT LINE CONSOLIDATION, MARKET+ GROWTH

It'll be worthwhile exploring a reduction in the portfolio. This has to be done with caution though i.e. we cannot lose the categories that in the transactions for our top customers.

A Market + 3% growth should remain our goal for the top 4 catetories. Growth in the bottom should be gravy i.e. we can choose to not invest in that growth.

Order Processing Times

Average Order Processing Times by State (Geo) of purchase

The first graph shows the processing time (Order entry to delivery) for the states that have the biggest average processing times. The graph goes from highest to lowest (top to bottom).

The average processing times range from ~400 hrs to ~ 600 hrs on this graph. 500 is what we think is a respectable time for most of our customer (considering the average across products and geographies). We should investigate what's leading to higher delays in states that show "Red".

Most worrisome delays

States from CE to MA are close to 500 hrs so there might be some low hanging fruits that bring us below the magic number.

States from PA to AP should get a lot of analysis with the aim of either explaining the delays or eliminating them.

Within States, what products are the bottleneck?

The delays may not be caused by all products in a state, so its worth looking at products that are the bottleneck. Clicking on a state(bar) gets us that detail.

The products in 2nd chart are arranged by the average processing times (Averages with in a state). Clicking on AP shows that "Sports_leisure" is the worst offender. "Agro" and "Telephony" are actually doing fine there.

A thing to note (by clicking at the bottom of states graph) is that states with good averages have pretty much all the products doing fine with timing. There's no wide variances. Its only the "Red" states that have some products that are getting delayed.

ACTIONS: INVESTIGATE WORST PRODUCT PROCESSING TIMES

The take away here would be to eliminate the worst delays for individual products in states where they are impacted. One way to get started would be to compare these products in problem states with states where they are not facing these delays.

Seeing less of "Red" on this chart could also be a metric.
